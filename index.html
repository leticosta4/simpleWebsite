The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo

Taylor Jenkings Reid

'The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo' follows aging Hollywood icon Evelyn Hugo as she reveals her scandalous life story to unknown magazine reporter Monique Grant. Monique, struggling in her personal and professional life, is surprised by Evelyn's choice and is determined to use this opportunity to kickstart her career. As Evelyn recounts her journey from the 1950s to the 1980s, including seven marriages and unexpected friendships, Monique becomes deeply connected to the star. However, as the story unfolds, it becomes evident that Evelyn's life is intertwined with Monique's in tragic and irreversible ways. Despite what the title may suggest, this novel doesn't really focus on the husbands, but rather Evelyn herself, where we quickly learn that there is much more to her than meets the eye.

They Both Die at The End

Adam Silvera

On September 5, a little after midnight, Death-Cast calls Mateo Torrez and Rufus Emeterio to give them some bad news: They’re going to die today. Mateo and Rufus are total strangers, but, for different reasons, they’re both looking to make a new friend on their End Day. The good news: There’s an app for that. It’s called the Last Friend, and through it, Rufus and Mateo are about to meet up for one last great adventure—to live a lifetime in a single day. Throughout their day, they grapple with the imminent reality of their deaths, forming an intense bond and experiencing a range of emotions. 'They Both Die At The End' showcases elements such as compelling storytelling, realistic portrayal of emotions, and thought-provoking exploration of deep themes, like the value of living in the present moment.

Jantar Secreto

Raphael Montes

A group of young people leaves a small town in Paraná to live in Rio de Janeiro. They rent an apartment in Copacabana and do their best to pay for college and keep their dreams of success alive in the capital city. But money is tight and rent is overdue. To get out of the hole and maintain the apartment, the friends adopt an unorthodox strategy: raising funds through secret dinners, advertised online to an exclusive clientele of the Rio elite. On the menu: human flesh. From there, they become involved in a spiral of crimes, uncovering a network of body smuggling, clandestine slaughterhouses, and eccentric high society, pushing the limits of a perverse nature they never imagined existed in each of them. The book offers insightful critiques and portrays Brazilian society very well. 'Jantar Secreto' is an irresistible read that grips you from the first page, with a plot full of mystery, suspense, and twists.

Crooked Plow

Itamar Vieira Jr.

Bibiana and Belonísia are daughters of workers on a farm in the hinterlands of Bahia, descendants of slaves for whom abolition has never been more than a date marked on the calendar. Intrigued by a mysterious suitcase under their grandmother's bed, they pay for their daring with an accident that will forever change their lives, making them so dependent that one will even be the voice of the other. However, as the years go by, their closeness will unravel with each one's perspective on their surroundings: while Belonísia seems content with farm work and the charms of their father, amid candles and incense, Bibiana perceives from an early age the injustice of the servitude imposed on the family for three decades and decides to fight for land rights and workers' emancipation. In a plot woven with ancient secrets and in the shadow of inequalities that still linger in Brazil today, "Torto Arado" is a novel with multiple perspectives, beautiful and moving, telling a story of life and death, struggle and redemption, of characters who have crossed time without ever managing to escape anonymity.

A Thousand Splendid Suns

Khaled Hosseini

Mariam is only fifteen when she is sent to Kabul to marry the bitter Rasheed, who is thirty years her senior. Nearly two decades later, in growing unrest, the same tragedy strikes fifteen-year-old Laila, who must leave her home and join Mariam's unhappy household. Laila and Mariam find consolation in each other, their friendship growing so deep and strong that they become like family to one another and help each other overcome the challenges they face. As time passes, the Taliban begins to dominate Afghanistan, the streets of Kabul loud with the sound of gunfire and bombs, life becoming a desperate struggle against starvation, brutality, and fear, women's endurance tested beyond their worst imaginings. However, love can move people to act in unexpected ways, leading them to overcome the most daunting obstacles with surprising heroism. "A Thousand Splendid Suns" portrays a wounded country and a story of family and friendship, of an unforgiving time, an unlikely bond, and an indestructible love, a book that continues to captivate and move readers worldwide.

Normal People

Sally Rooney

At school, Connell and Marianne act as if they don't know each other. Connell is popular and well-adjusted, while Marianne is lonely, proud, and private. However, when Connell picks up his mother from her job at Marianne's house, a strong connection develops between them. A year later, they both studying at Trinity College in Dublin. Marianne thrives in the social scene, while Connell hangs back, feeling shy and uncertain. Over the years, they orbit each other, straying toward other people and possibilities but always magnetically, irresistibly drawn back together. As Marianne faces self-destructive tendencies and Connell searches for meaning elsewhere, they must confront how far they're willing to go to save each other. 'Normal People' explores themes such as friendship, love, identity, social class, and mental health, providing an insightful perspective on the complexities of human relationships.
